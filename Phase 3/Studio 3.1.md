Historical Theme: Art connected to survival, ritual, and belief
Historical Period: Paleolithic
Artwork Inspiration: Pech Merle Cave
Prompt:
Create a cave painting on a rough rock wall inspired by the Pech Merle Cave in France. Draw animals like horses or bison using colors such as black, red, and brown. Add handprints and simple shapes to make it look like early humans created it. The wall should appear bumpy and uneven, and the drawings should be simple yet clear. Focus on line, shape, texture, and contrast so the animals stand out.
This artwork was created because people during the Paleolithic period used art for survival and beliefs. They relied on animals for food. So, they probably made these paintings as part of hunting rituals or to bring good luck. This reflects their culture and religion, as they believed that art could help them survive.
Visual Prompt 2
Historical Theme: Fertility, procreation, and the body
Historical Period: Paleolithic
Artwork Inspiration: Venus of Laussel
Prompt:
Create a stone carving (relief sculpture) inspired by the Venus of Laussel. Show a female figure carved into rock with a rounded stomach, wide hips, and one arm holding a horn with lines on it. The surface should look rough like real stone, and the figure should stand out. Focus on texture, shape, and emphasis so the body is the main focus.
The purpose of this artwork is to show fertility and having children, which was really important back then. People in the Paleolithic period needed their groups to grow so they could survive. This artwork shows their beliefs and culture because they made art that focused on life and reproduction.
